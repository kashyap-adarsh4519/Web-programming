Introduction to Chhath Pooja

Chhath Pooja is a significant Hindu festival dedicated to the Sun God (Surya) and Chhathi Maiya. It is observed with great devotion and enthusiasm in various parts of India, especially in the states of Bihar, Jharkhand, and Uttar Pradesh.

History and Significance

The history of Chhath Pooja dates back to ancient times and is mentioned in several Hindu scriptures. It is believed that Draupadi and the Pandavas performed this pooja to regain their lost kingdom. The festival is observed to thank the Sun God for sustaining life on Earth and to seek his blessings for prosperity and well-being.

Rituals and Practices

The festival spans four days and involves several rituals such as holy bathing, fasting, and offering prayers to the setting and rising sun. Devotees gather at riverbanks and water bodies to perform the rituals, accompanied by singing traditional songs.

  1. Nahay Khay: The first day begins with a ritualistic bath and preparation of pure vegetarian food.
  2. Lohanda and Kharna: The second day involves fasting and offering homemade sweets to the deities.
  3. Sandhya Arghya: The third day is marked by offering prayers to the setting sun.
  4. Usha Arghya: The fourth and final day involves offering prayers to the rising sun.

Chhath Pooja Celebrations

Chhath Pooja is celebrated with great fervor and joy. People clean and decorate their homes, prepare special dishes, and gather with family and friends to celebrate the festival. The atmosphere is filled with a sense of communal harmony and spiritual fervor.
